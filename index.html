About

Hello, my name is Yash Patel. I am a fifteen year old programmer from California. Since a young age, I have always enjoyed building things, a passion which began at the young age of two years old when I was introduced to Legos which quickly began a favorite pastime of mine for years. Fast forward to the present, I still have not forgotten my passion for creation although now my work is done through a different medium, programming. Since discovering the world of programming at about the age of 8, I have spent countless hours programming a variety of different types of projects and pursuing my passions in the realm of computers from building PCs to creating digital art. I can code in multiple different languages including Python, C#, Java, SQL, and JavaScript.

Web of Death Game

This is a video game that I made for the annual Boss Rush Game Jam. Of the 7,000 people that joined the competition and the 863 games that ended up being finished and submitted on time, my game got 86st place in art which is a considerable amount of progress from previously when I did not do as well. This game was also completed mostly under the course of one weekend as I learned about the competition very late but still wanted to participate despite it almost being over which I believe makes a testament to the progress I have made as a game developer. This game was made in the Unity Game Engine and is coded mainly using C#.

Robotics Team Robot Subsytems

In my high school's robotics team for the FIRST Robotics League, I helped program multiple subsystems for the robot including an elevator to help place objects as well as the intake subsystem which helps the robot collect items to compete in the game. These are crucial subsystems as without them, we would not be able to score any points and would immediately lose the tournament. These subsystems were coded in Java.
